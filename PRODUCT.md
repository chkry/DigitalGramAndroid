# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who journal for personal reflection, mindfulness, and emotional processing. They open the app in quiet moments: end of day on the couch, morning with coffee, a pause between tasks. The job is to capture a thought before it fades, not to organize a knowledge base. They value privacy, simplicity, and the feeling that the app respects their time. Most entries are short; the act of writing matters more than the output.

## Product Purpose

DigitalGram is a daily journal that mirrors the simplicity of pen and paper. One entry per day, markdown for those who want it, nothing forced. It exists because most note apps optimize for volume and structure; DigitalGram optimizes for the habit of showing up. Success looks like someone opening it every day without thinking about it, the way you'd reach for a favorite notebook.

## Brand Personality

Calm, minimal, warm. Like a well-made notebook you carry everywhere. The interface should feel quiet and confident, never loud or decorative. Warmth comes from texture and tone, not from illustration or animation. The app recedes so the writing stays front and center.

## Anti-references

- **Social media apps**: No feeds, likes, sharing, follower counts, or engagement mechanics. This is private, full stop.
- **Generic note apps (Notion, Evernote)**: No databases, toggles, page trees, or feature sprawl. DigitalGram is deliberately simple; complexity is a bug.
- **Corporate/enterprise tools**: No sterile dashboards, data tables, or admin-panel chrome. This is personal, not professional.
- **Overly cute/gamified apps**: No streaks, badges, achievements, confetti, or cartoon illustrations. The reward is the journal itself.

## Design Principles

1. **Disappear into the writing.** Every UI element should justify its presence. If removing it doesn't hurt the writing experience, remove it.
2. **One thing per moment.** Don't show the timeline and the editor at once. Don't stack settings and content. Each screen has one job.
3. **Warmth without decoration.** Use color temperature, spacing, and typography to create comfort. Never add ornament for its own sake.
4. **Respect the ritual.** Opening the app should feel like opening a notebook, not booting a tool. Speed, quietness, and predictability matter.
5. **Honest simplicity.** Features exist because users need them, not because competitors have them. Say no to feature creep.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast ratios across all 20+ themes
- Respect system font size preferences
- Support for reduced motion (no gratuitous animation)
- Color-blind safe accent choices across theme palette
- Screen reader compatibility for timeline navigation and markdown preview
